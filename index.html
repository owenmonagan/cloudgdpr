About

The GDPR (General Data Protection Regulation) seeks to create a harmonized data protection law framework across the European Union and aims to give back to data subjects, control of their personal data, whilst imposing strict rules on those hosting and processing this data, anywhere in the world.

Any U.S. organization doing business and/or gathering data from the European Union must be GDPR compliant.

A data protection officer (DPO) is an enterprise security leadership role required by the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR). Data protection officers are responsible for overseeing data protection strategy and implementation to ensure compliance with GDPR requirements.

Our team contains experienced data protection officers and data engineers. Our services are tailored for organisations needing expert assistance from professionals who can explain concepts at a high level all the way down to real word implementation techniques.

Model

Our model will ensure your organisation can achieve compliance in-house. It is repeatable and applicable to existing and new projects, products and processes. Contact us now to get started.

Accountability - Become General Data Protection Regulation fluent. Understand the scope, liability, fines and roles of the regulation. Differentiate between data, personal data and sensitive personal data. Use our helpful real word examples to relate to your organisational needs.

Risk Assessment - Carry out a series of assessments to ensure your organisation won't be blindsided. Evaluate your ability to respond to requirements of the GDPR. Priorities risks in relation to legal consequences.

Compliance - Ensuring your organisation has a lawful basis for its data processes. Review and amend existing contracts. Acquire or develop Data Processing Agreements. Implement technical and processes changes. Make use of our in-depth technical solutions to common data processing issues.

Data Protection by Default - Compliance is continuous. Reimplement the model for future projects. Save time by using our compliance document structure. Understand the value of Data Protection Officers. Establish relationships with relevant legal bodies.
